EKG ANALYSIS — LEAD II
RHYTHM TYPE
ST-ELEVATION MI (STEMI)
Pro-Tip: Notice the "Fireman's Hat" appearance. The ST segment (the line after the tall spike) does not return to the baseline. It stays elevated.
CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE
⚠ CRITICAL — MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION
This indicates an acute blockage of a coronary artery. Tissue is actively dying. Action: Check troponin, give Aspirin, and notify cardiology for immediate cath lab activation.
